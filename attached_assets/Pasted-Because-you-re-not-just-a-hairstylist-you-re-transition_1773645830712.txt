Because you’re not just a hairstylist — you’re transitioning into founder / author / visionary creative — I would not make the brand too flashy. I’d make it feel like:

editorial luxury + intelligent creativity + premium restraint

So I’d suggest this as a core palette:

Core palette
	•	Obsidian Black — #111111
	•	Antique Gold — #B08D57
	•	White Gold — #D8D1C5
	•	Deep Jade — #145B4B
	•	Soft Jade Mist — #C7D9D2

This gives you:
	•	seriousness
	•	elegance
	•	freshness
	•	creativity
	•	room to scale across multiple brands/assets

How to use them

For websites

Use:
	•	Black / charcoal for background or headers
	•	Warm white / white gold for main content areas
	•	Gold for buttons, dividers, icons, micro-details
	•	Jade for highlights, hover states, special sections, quote boxes, category markers

A luxury website usually looks better when the jade is used as a signature accent, not a dominant fill color everywhere.

For Instagram

Use:
	•	black as anchor
	•	gold for typography details or linework
	•	jade as a recurring brand cue
	•	white gold as negative space or text support

A really strong IG feed could alternate:
	•	dark editorial posts
	•	clean light quote/announcement posts
	•	jade-accent educational or founder posts

That would feel branded without looking repetitive.

For packaging

Use:
	•	matte black base
	•	gold foil or printed gold accents
	•	jade inner packaging / tissue / label detail
	•	white gold for secondary text or cool minimal panels

That would look very premium.

For author branding

For author materials, I’d tone the metallic feeling down slightly:
	•	black
	•	warm white
	•	jade
	•	restrained gold

Too much metallic in author branding can feel overly decorative. A more restrained version looks more credible and premium.

What not to do

Avoid:
	•	bright emerald mixed with muted jade
	•	neon green undertones
	•	yellow gold and cool silver battling equally
	•	too many gradients
	•	using black, gold, silver, and jade all at full intensity in one layout
	•	glossy everything

Luxury is usually about restraint + spacing + contrast + finish.

A smart brand system for you

Because you do multiple things, you may want a master palette with slightly different emphasis by brand area:

Personal / hairstylist brand
	•	black
	•	white gold
	•	jade
	•	subtle gold

Product brand
	•	black
	•	gold
	•	jade
	•	minimal silver

Author brand
	•	soft black
	•	warm white
	•	jade
	•	restrained champagne gold

That way the whole ecosystem feels related, but not identical.

My honest opinion

Yes, this can work beautifully.

In fact, it can look more original and elevated than standard luxury palettes, because the jade introduces identity and freshness. The trick is making jade your signature, not your competition to gold.

So the formula is:

Black leads. Gold elevates. Silver refines. Jade distinguishes.

That’s the sweet spot.
