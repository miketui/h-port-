Your revised structure is better because:
	•	the hero gets to feel intentional and cinematic
	•	the 4-image section becomes proof of range
	•	the page feels more editorial and less like a template slider
	•	you can still showcase celebrity, beauty, red carpet, and commercial variety early

Best luxury behavior

Section 1: Hero

Use one image only for the opening screen.

It should do the emotional work:
	•	premium
	•	fashion-forward
	•	memorable
	•	high-value
	•	calm confidence

This should be your strongest single image, not necessarily the most famous person.

Section 2: Mini carousel / image strip

As the user scrolls slightly, reveal a 4-image controlled display.

This should do the proof work:
	•	range
	•	authority
	•	category variety
	•	client caliber
	•	visual depth

How to make it feel luxurious

Hero section

Keep it clean:
	•	one strong image
	•	minimal copy
	•	elegant type
	•	restrained CTA
	•	no clutter
	•	no heavy UI controls

4-image section

This should not feel like a cheap rotating widget.

Better directions:
	•	horizontal editorial strip
	•	4-up image panel
	•	slow-moving carousel with minimal controls
	•	grid/slider hybrid

Luxury cues:
	•	generous spacing
	•	subtle motion only
	•	strong cropping consistency
	•	no loud arrows or dots
	•	smooth transition behavior

Best content strategy for the 4 images

Use the 4-image section to show four distinct strengths.

A strong mix would be:
	1.	editorial
	2.	red carpet
	3.	beauty
	4.	commercial / ecom

That immediately tells the visitor:
	•	you have range
	•	you have taste
	•	you have polish
	•	you are not one-note

My recommendation on carousel style

For your site, I would lean toward one of these:

Best option

4 visible images in a luxury horizontal panel, with slight motion or hover behavior

Why:
	•	feels more modern/editorial
	•	users see range instantly
	•	less template-like than a standard mini slider

Second-best option

mini carousel that shows 1.2–1.5 images on mobile and 4 images on desktop

Why:
	•	preserves responsiveness
	•	still feels curated if motion is subtle

Less ideal

traditional carousel with:
	•	obvious arrows
	•	dots
	•	fast autoplay
	•	cramped spacing

That tends to cheapen luxury sites.

Best page flow

A strong homepage sequence would be:
	1.	Hero image + headline + short positioning statement
	2.	4-image luxury image rail / mini carousel
	3.	featured work / categories
	4.	press / credibility
	5.	about / booking CTA

That flow is much more premium than putting everything into the hero.
